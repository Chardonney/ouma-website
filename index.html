Many moons ago, a beautiful lady was born. The old ones said that before she came, people didn't know what to call that warm feeling in their chests. She grew up to be a very compassionate woman who shows affection to those dear to her. Wherever she walked, cold and dusty rooms grew warm & worried hearts grew quiet. Grandpa saw it first and could never keep his eyes from her ever again. Legend has it that she knows the cure about just anything. Some might go so far as to call her a "doctor" or "nurse". It is not asburb to believe, since eye-witnesses said that her heart was stitched with rooibos steam and Sunday prayers. She could heal a scraped knee with a single kiss, and mend an ice cold day with a delicious pot of soup. She even showed her grandchildren how to knit wonderful scarfs - which later turned into a family project of giving to the elderly in need. Kings had no crown like the deep love she wore - and no tall castle as safe as her lap. With hands that bakes the best milk tart and whose cooking for every meal is chef-kiss worthy (except when covered in oddly black crusts for being too long in the oven). She loves deeply, laugh loudly, cherishes every moment and stood beside Grandpa through every season. Now she carries the same love for all of us!

This is all to say: Happy Birthday Ouma!
